Leadership Philosophy

I found early on in my technology career that to build great relationships with our customers, we had to do the little things right. When working in desktop support, this meant going the extra mile for them. If you replace their computer, you put their files back where they were before. When you setup a new computer, you made sure to cable manage their desk for them. As a Systems Specialist, doing the little things right means being good stewards of the University of Wyoming's data. This quote from Admiral McRaven is an ever standing reminder that every task we do for our customers is a chance to reinforce that our organization is there to help them, not get in the way of progress towards their or the universities mission.

Code Of Ethics

My code of ethics was written during my LDR-5203 Leadership Ethics Class. Its main goal was to make sure that IT personel remember customer service is important in all IT career paths.

The Tenets

  • One will always serve and protect the best interest of clients/customers, their data, privacy, and technology resources.
  • One will be transparent, open, and accountable for actions taken in an IT capacity.
  • One will listen to all ideas from peers, customers, and clients alike when making IT decisions.

Clifton Strengths
